# Spherid / Drift Chapter Invocation Sheet

**Status:** Writing sheet  
**Date:** 2026-04-24  
**Purpose:** Establish a short ceremonial narrative voice for openings, chapter transitions, threshold beats, and rare title-card text.

---

## 1. Voice Rule

These invocations should be:
- brief,
- spare,
- symbolic,
- non-explanatory,
- resonant with movement and shape philosophy.

They should not become:
- verbose narration,
- lore exposition,
- frequent text overlays.

Best use:
- opening of the game,
- act transitions,
- major threshold crossings,
- revelation prefaces,
- final chapter entry.

---

## 2. Core Opening

Use this as the current strongest opening pair:

- **The pilgrim is a spherid.**
- **Its name is Drift.**

This is likely the foundational title-card language for the project.

---

## 3. Alternative Opening Variants

If variants are needed, these are close in tone:

- The pilgrim is a spherid. Its name is Drift.
- A spherid woke beneath the roots. Its name was Drift.
- The world had many forms. This one was a spherid. Its name was Drift.
- The pilgrim was small. The pilgrim was a spherid. Its name was Drift.

Recommendation:
keep the original pair. It is stronger than the variants because of its simplicity.

---

## 4. Chapter Invocations By Biome

## Moss Cathedral

- The pilgrim is a spherid.
- Its name is Drift.

Alternative lines:
- Drift woke where the roots kept stillness.
- The first shelter was round and soft with dew.

## Marble Dunes

- The stone opened into light.
- Drift learned the joy of the line.

Alternative lines:
- The earth curved and asked to be trusted.
- A spherid can listen with its weight.

## Sand Choir

- The oloid crossed the dunes.
- Drift learned that motion could be written.

Alternative lines:
- The sand remembered every passing form.
- A circle became a trace.

## Glass Forest

- A tricylinder waited at the threshold.
- Drift learned that not every passage yields to force.

Alternative lines:
- Some doors open only to restraint.
- The bright surface heard too much.

## Bloom Caverns

- Below the roots, the world began to answer.
- Drift learned to listen back.

Alternative lines:
- The blooms kept a deeper rhythm.
- Not every voice was sound.

## River Mirrors

- The water gave back what the sky could not hold.
- Drift crossed lightly.

Alternative lines:
- Reflection became a path.
- A spherid can be carried without surrender.

## Wind Arches

- The seed remembered the sky.
- Drift learned to trust what carried it.

Alternative lines:
- The high paths were never meant for force.
- Lift begins where fear loosens.

## The Inner Sea

- The world gathered its languages into one.
- Drift moved without asking.

Alternative lines:
- What had been path became fluency.
- The last threshold was not a gate but a motion.

## 5. Shape Presence Invocations

These can be used for optional discovery moments or symbolic transitions.

### Meander Pilgrim

- The oloid crossed the dunes.
- It did not hurry.

### Facet Keeper

- A tricylinder waited at the threshold.
- It changed without moving far.

### Seed Ascendant

- The seed remembered the sky.
- Drift had not yet learned its name.

### Great Carrier

- The old body slept beneath the path.
- The world still knew how to carry weight.

---

## 6. Revelation Invocations

These are for the rare altered states.

### First Revelation

- The hidden pattern did not begin in light.
- It began in touch.

### Second Revelation

- The wind was not empty.
- It had always been shaped.

### Final Revelation

- Nothing had been separate.
- Drift only learned how to see it.

---

## 7. Ending Invocations

Possible closing lines:

- Drift did not leave the world. It learned how to move with it.
- The pilgrim remained a spherid. The path did not remain a path.
- The world answered softly. Drift answered in motion.
- What had drifted began at last to belong.

Recommendation:
the strongest current ending line is likely:

- **What had drifted began at last to belong.**

---

## 8. Usage Recommendation

Do not use all of these.

Best practice:
- one opening pair,
- one line or pair per major act,
- one or two revelation lines,
- one ending line.

The power of this language depends on restraint.
