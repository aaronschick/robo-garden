# Urchin Game Concept: Demo Slice Plan

**Status:** Planning plan  
**Date:** 2026-04-24  
**Purpose:** Define a practical first playable vertical slice that proves the game’s identity without requiring the entire world or full late-game systems.

---

## 1. Slice Goal

The first vertical slice should prove five things:

1. Rolling traversal feels good.
2. The world looks distinct and beautiful in a stylized way.
3. Silent environmental storytelling works.
4. Resonance interaction is compelling.
5. The game can alternate tenderness, flow, and wonder within a compact runtime.

It should **not** try to prove the entire game.

Do not build:
- all biomes,
- full late-game vehicles,
- the whole revelation arc,
- every puzzle family,
- the whole symbolic system.

The slice should be narrow but identity-complete.

---

## 2. Recommended Slice Scope

Best slice structure:

**Moss Cathedral -> short Marble Dunes transition -> compact Sand Choir payoff**

Why this is the best slice:
- shows the opening tenderness,
- proves the joy of rolling,
- introduces resonance without overloading production,
- gives a clean emotional mini-arc,
- fits the visual style packet well,
- uses systems that are central to the full game.

This is better than starting in Wind Arches or building a broad late-game showcase first.

---

## 3. Slice Length

Target player runtime:
- 15 to 30 minutes for a polished linear demo,
- 30 to 45 minutes if light optional exploration is included.

That is enough to deliver:
- awakening,
- first movement joy,
- one tension beat,
- one scenic descent,
- first resonance discovery,
- one small symbolic reveal.

---

## 4. Slice Structure

## Segment 1: Awakening Basin

Biome:
- Moss Cathedral

Purpose:
- establish scale and tenderness,
- teach basic rolling,
- teach shelter and curiosity,
- deliver the “small being in huge world” feeling.

Key beats:
- waking in a moss basin,
- first roll,
- simple environmental nudge puzzle,
- brief hide-under-roots moment.

Systems needed:
- base movement,
- simple camera behavior,
- environmental response,
- soft atmospheric presentation.

## Segment 2: First Pulse

Biome:
- Moss Cathedral

Purpose:
- introduce intentional bodily action,
- let the player feel agency,
- unlock a small route.

Key beats:
- first pulse/compression moment,
- pulse used to move a soft obstacle or open a route,
- subtle emotional lift.

Systems needed:
- pulse mechanic,
- responsive organic prop,
- sound/FX feedback.

## Segment 3: Flow Descent

Biome:
- Marble Dunes transition

Purpose:
- prove the joy of motion,
- introduce line reading and momentum pleasure,
- show the world opening.

Key beats:
- emergence into sunlight,
- one beautiful descent sequence,
- one optional alternate line,
- scenic payoff shot.

Systems needed:
- polished rolling feel,
- terrain shaping for flow,
- dust FX and strong lighting.

## Segment 4: Buried Choir

Biome:
- compact Sand Choir chamber/zone

Purpose:
- introduce resonance as language,
- create the first “the world is listening” moment,
- show that movement can write into the environment.

Key beats:
- strange reactive sand surface,
- first visible ripple response,
- trace or reveal puzzle,
- buried structure wakes.

Systems needed:
- basic resonance,
- sand ripple feedback,
- symbolic/glyph guidance,
- one environmental reveal.

## Segment 5: Mini Payoff

Biome:
- Sand Choir reveal space

Purpose:
- deliver a clean end-note of wonder,
- hint at larger journey,
- end with invitation, not closure.

Key beats:
- a hidden bridge/path/structure blooms outward,
- distant glimpse of a shape presence or larger world,
- maybe one short symbolic image suggesting the pilgrimage continues.

Systems needed:
- reveal animation,
- one distant mystery element,
- strong final composition.

---

## 5. Systems To Include

Absolutely include:
- basic rolling traversal
- pulse/compression
- terrain flow in one controlled descent
- one resonance interaction mode
- glyph/symbol hints in environment
- atmospheric lighting and particles
- soft environmental storytelling

Include only in minimal form if possible:
- one simple hide/tension beat
- one optional side route
- one symbolic presence glimpse

Exclude from the first slice:
- water skimming
- grass-sail skiff
- glider systems
- full device roster
- Glass Forest precision systems
- Bloom Caverns vertical launch
- full revelation-state rendering stack

---

## 6. Visual Scope Recommendation

For the slice, fully polish only:
- Moss Cathedral material family
- Marble Dunes material family
- Sand Choir material family
- the urchin base look
- a small resonance FX set

This is enough to prove the art direction without exploding content scope.

---

## 7. Puzzle Scope Recommendation

Use only these puzzle families in the first slice:

1. Reveal
2. Route
3. Match
4. Trace

Do not try to introduce the whole grammar.

Ideal sequence:
- nudge / timing interaction
- pulse unlock
- scenic route read
- resonance reveal puzzle

That already communicates the game’s identity clearly.

---

## 8. Emotional Arc Of The Slice

The slice should feel like:

1. smallness
2. curiosity
3. first agency
4. delight in motion
5. mystery
6. wonder

That is enough to make people understand the project.

It should not try to hit:
- transcendence,
- full liberation,
- final ascent,
- large cast worldbuilding.

Those belong to the full game.

---

## 9. Success Criteria

The vertical slice is successful if players come away saying:

- “rolling feels beautiful”
- “this world feels unlike generic indie fantasy”
- “I understood what the game was emotionally”
- “the resonance mechanic feels special”
- “I want to see what other biomes do with this”

It is not necessary that the slice already demonstrates:
- large-scale complexity,
- late-game speed,
- all movement systems,
- full narrative resolution.

---

## 10. Recommended Optional Additions

If there is room, include one of these:

### Option A: Distant Meander Pilgrim Glimpse

Very high value for symbolic worldbuilding with low implementation burden.

### Option B: Short Sanctuary End Scene

A quiet final room or overlook after the Sand Choir reveal.

### Option C: One Tiny Wonder Route

An optional side path showing that the world rewards curiosity, not just progression.

Do not include all three unless scope is unusually healthy.

---

## 11. Main Production Risk

The biggest risk is trying to prove too much.

A vertical slice for this game should be:
- narrow,
- polished,
- emotionally coherent,
- visually authored,
- mechanically clear.

Not feature complete.

---

## 12. Final Recommendation

Build the first slice as:

**Awakening in Moss Cathedral, first pulse, first flow descent through Marble Dunes, first resonance reveal in Sand Choir, ending on a note of wonder.**

That is the smallest slice that still feels like the real game.
