# Urchin Game Concept: Biome Playable Content Breakdown

**Status:** Planning breakdown  
**Date:** 2026-04-24  
**Purpose:** Define the playable content role of each biome so the full game has a clear balance of traversal, puzzle grammar, symbolism, emotional pacing, and production scope.

---

## 1. Usage

This document is for:
- level planning,
- pacing review,
- scope control,
- feature distribution,
- biome-specific system prioritization.

It answers:
- what each biome is for,
- what the player learns there,
- what kind of content should dominate,
- what emotional note it should leave behind.

---

## 2. Content Types

Each biome can draw from these content types:

- traversal teaching
- scenic flow run
- resonance reveal
- trace puzzle
- timing/match puzzle
- restraint/stealth tension
- vertical route challenge
- carrier/device introduction
- symbolic encounter
- sanctuary/rest beat
- climax sequence

No biome should try to carry every content type equally.

---

## 3. Moss Cathedral

### Main Job

Teach intimacy with the body and world.

### Dominant Content

- traversal teaching
- sanctuary exploration
- simple nudge/timing puzzles
- first vulnerability beats

### Secondary Content

- first hidden side alcoves
- first symbolic/glyph exposure
- first shelter/hide sequence

### Player Learns

- basic rolling feel
- terrain reading at low stakes
- how to use shelter
- how the world responds softly
- first pulse/compression action

### Emotional Note

- tenderness
- curiosity
- smallness

### Scope Guidance

Keep geometry relatively contained and readable.
This biome does not need huge complexity. It needs:
- strong atmosphere,
- clean teaching,
- emotional anchoring.

---

## 4. Marble Dunes

### Main Job

Prove that rolling can feel ecstatic.

### Dominant Content

- scenic flow runs
- route reading
- momentum puzzles
- optional mastery lines

### Secondary Content

- chime/impact route triggers
- broad vistas
- symbolic traces that imply larger pilgrimage

### Player Learns

- carve behavior
- line choice
- momentum preservation
- speed trust

### Emotional Note

- exhilaration
- elegance
- sunlight and expansion

### Scope Guidance

This biome should not get clogged with too many puzzle gimmicks.
Its primary job is to let the player feel joy in movement.

---

## 5. Sand Choir

### Main Job

Introduce resonance as language.

### Dominant Content

- resonance reveal puzzles
- trace puzzles
- buried path activations
- symbolic environmental storytelling

### Secondary Content

- meandering route hints
- distant shape-presence foreshadowing
- ceremonial puzzle spaces

### Player Learns

- resonance/vibration emission
- visible pattern-writing
- glyphs as movement instruction
- the world can be awakened

### Emotional Note

- mystery
- wonder
- discovery

### Scope Guidance

This is a concept-heavy biome. Keep the puzzle grammar focused:
- reveal,
- trace,
- conduct.

Do not overload it with too many unrelated puzzle ideas.

---

## 6. Glass Forest

### Main Job

Teach discipline, restraint, and threshold seriousness.

### Dominant Content

- restraint puzzles
- fine resonance control challenges
- acoustic stealth or listening tension
- threshold route spaces

### Secondary Content

- precision traversal
- tri-radial symbolic encounters
- brief predator or sound-risk beats

### Player Learns

- not all spaces reward force
- resonance can be too much
- stillness and softness matter
- orientation and choice matter

### Emotional Note

- fragility
- tension
- awe through danger

### Scope Guidance

Keep this biome relatively concise compared to Sand Choir or Wind Arches.
It should sharpen the game’s language, not dominate the experience.

---

## 7. Bloom Caverns

### Main Job

Deepen resonance into intimacy and prepare the first revelation.

### Dominant Content

- rhythmic organic puzzles
- living environment interactions
- vertical route unlocks
- sacred chamber progression

### Secondary Content

- sanctuary beats
- symbolic listening motifs
- revelation staging

### Player Learns

- resonance can be relational
- cadence matters deeply
- elastic launch / vertical travel
- the world’s systems are alive

### Emotional Note

- intimacy
- reverence
- sacred hidden order

### Scope Guidance

This biome should have strong atmosphere and fewer but richer puzzle spaces.
It is one of the emotional centers of the game.

---

## 8. River Mirrors

### Main Job

Broaden space and introduce serenity through graceful traversal.

### Dominant Content

- water-skimming sequences
- reflective route reading
- calm reveal puzzles
- first larger-scale movement chaining

### Secondary Content

- first real carrier/device relationship
- visual exhale after Bloom Caverns
- optional poetic side routes

### Player Learns

- skim logic
- reflection as guidance
- broader fluid route composition
- the world is now answering more clearly

### Emotional Note

- serenity
- poise
- relief

### Scope Guidance

This biome should not feel puzzle-dense.
It should feel open, graceful, and breathable.

---

## 9. Wind Arches

### Main Job

Deliver high-speed liberation and late-game trust.

### Dominant Content

- launch sequences
- sail/glide route chains
- wind alignment puzzles
- dramatic scenic traversal

### Secondary Content

- ascendant symbolism
- optional mastery lines
- second revelation setup

### Player Learns

- air-assisted movement trust
- wind route reading
- device/carrier mastery at scale
- freedom can be learned

### Emotional Note

- exhilaration
- daring
- release

### Scope Guidance

This biome needs room to breathe.
It should be one of the largest content spaces because late-game freedom needs time to feel real.

---

## 10. The Inner Sea

### Main Job

Synthesize all prior movement languages into the final act.

### Dominant Content

- full-flow traversal chains
- integrated multi-system sequences
- final symbolic route activation
- climactic movement set pieces

### Secondary Content

- distilled final sanctuaries
- unified glyph logic
- final world-scale alignment events

### Player Learns

- nothing brand new mechanically
- everything becomes coherent
- all prior verbs belong together

### Emotional Note

- belonging
- uplift
- freedom

### Scope Guidance

This biome should be cleaner and more distilled than earlier spaces.
Its power comes from synthesis, not from maximal density.

---

## 11. Content Balance Summary

Recommended emphasis by phase:

### Early Game

- traversal teaching
- sanctuary exploration
- one or two simple puzzles
- one strong scenic run

### Mid Game

- resonance discovery
- puzzle grammar deepening
- symbolism
- alternating tension and wonder

### Late Game

- broad route spaces
- carriers and devices
- scenic mastery runs
- less friction, more synthesis

---

## 12. Recommended Production Priority

If building progressively, the likely order of content focus is:

1. Moss Cathedral core traversal
2. Marble Dunes flow routes
3. Sand Choir resonance interactions
4. Bloom Caverns sacred puzzle spaces
5. River Mirrors skim routes
6. Wind Arches sail/glide chains
7. Glass Forest refinement spaces
8. The Inner Sea final synthesis

This order is not strict story order. It is a pragmatic build order based on core identity.

---

## 13. Recommendation

Treat each biome as a **movement chapter**, not just a visual setting.

The player should be able to say of every biome:
- what it taught,
- how it felt,
- what movement it celebrated,
- what symbolic truth it introduced.

That is what will make the game feel authored rather than assembled.
