# Spherid / Drift Game Concept: Image Generation Batch Runbook

**Status:** Planning runbook  
**Date:** 2026-04-24  
**Purpose:** Turn the image generation batch into a practical sequence of generation passes, evaluation rules, and selection criteria for building a usable concept image library.

---

## 1. Purpose

The earlier image-generation batch defines *what* to generate.

This runbook defines:
- in what order to generate images,
- how many to generate per pass,
- what to evaluate,
- what to keep,
- how to iterate without drifting from the style.

This is useful because concept generation can otherwise become:
- random,
- redundant,
- over-stylized,
- tonally incoherent,
- disconnected from production needs.

---

## 2. Generation Phases

## Phase 1: North Star Lock

Goal:
- lock the overall world style before exploring too many specifics.

Generate:
- 3 to 5 global environment images
- 2 to 3 “small character in vast world” images
- 1 to 2 material/shape composite sheets

Success criteria:
- the world feels painterly-sculptural,
- the atmosphere feels sincere rather than generic fantasy,
- the style clearly avoids realism,
- the world already looks traversable.

Do not move on until this phase feels coherent.

---

## Phase 2: Drift Character Lock

Goal:
- lock Drift's silhouette, material feel, and emotional readability.

Generate:
- 3 base character explorations
- 2 vulnerable-state images
- 2 joyful rolling-state images
- 2 resonance-state images

Success criteria:
- character remains iconic and simple,
- non-facial expression reads clearly,
- shell material supports multiple biomes,
- resonance states feel special but still restrained.

---

## Phase 3: Early Biome Anchors

Goal:
- define the visual feel of the MVP biomes.

Generate:
- 3 Moss Cathedral images
- 3 Marble Dunes images
- 3 Sand Choir images

Success criteria:
- each biome is clearly distinct,
- all still belong to one world,
- early slice mood is legible,
- traversal lines are visible in the art.

This is one of the most important phases.

---

## Phase 4: Symbol And Shape Presence Exploration

Goal:
- define the larger world language without overbuilding the cast.

Generate:
- 2 glyph/symbol sheets
- 2 Meander Pilgrim images
- 2 Facet Keeper images
- 2 Seed Ascendant images

Success criteria:
- symbolic language feels movement-derived,
- shape presences feel meaningful, not random,
- the world becomes deeper without turning into conventional fantasy lore.

---

## Phase 5: Carrier And Device Exploration

Goal:
- visualize the essential vehicle/device roster.

Generate:
- 2 Water Skimmer concepts
- 2 Grass-Sail Skiff concepts
- 2 Seed Glider concepts
- 2 Resonance Rail or Lift Bloom concepts

Success criteria:
- devices feel sacred, not industrial,
- carriers feel like extensions of the same world language,
- they imply movement styles clearly.

---

## Phase 6: Revelation-State Reserve

Goal:
- define the visual ceiling of the project without contaminating the baseline style.

Generate:
- 2 Bloom revelation images
- 2 Wind revelation images
- 1 Inner Sea final vision image

Success criteria:
- revelation images are unmistakably elevated,
- they remain clean and readable,
- they do not look like a different game.

Use this phase sparingly.

---

## 3. Per-Image Evaluation Questions

Every generated image should be evaluated with the same questions:

1. Does it belong to the game we are making?
2. Is the silhouette structure strong?
3. Is the scene readable for traversal or symbolic interpretation?
4. Is it stylized in an intentional way rather than merely low-detail?
5. Does it preserve emotional sincerity?
6. Would this image help future production rather than merely inspire?

If the answer to 4 or 6 is “no,” discard it.

---

## 4. Keep / Reject Rules

## Keep Images That:

- feel painterly-sculptural,
- have strong route readability,
- use atmosphere well,
- make materials feel distinct without realism,
- support the project’s tone,
- show clear value hierarchy,
- suggest gameplay as well as mood.

## Reject Images That:

- drift into photorealism,
- feel like generic fantasy wallpaper,
- are overcluttered,
- lose the small-character scale story,
- make vehicles or devices too mechanical,
- make revelation states look chaotic rather than sacred,
- make the urchin too mascot-like or too sci-fi.

---

## 5. Recommended Image Counts

To stay efficient:

### Core lock set

- 5 global style images
- 6 to 8 urchin images
- 9 early biome anchors

### Secondary set

- 6 shape/glyph images
- 8 device/carrier images

### Rare set

- 5 revelation images

Total target:
- roughly 30 to 40 useful images

That is enough to build a coherent concept library without drowning in variants.

---

## 6. Naming And Curation Suggestions

If generated assets are later stored, use folders like:

- `00_world_north_star`
- `01_urchin_character`
- `02_moss_cathedral`
- `03_marble_dunes`
- `04_sand_choir`
- `05_shapes_glyphs`
- `06_devices_carriers`
- `07_revelations`
- `08_rejected_but_useful`

Also tag images by:
- shape
- material
- mood
- gameplay usefulness

This makes the image set useful beyond inspiration.

---

## 7. Iteration Strategy

If a batch is weak:

### If too realistic

Push toward:
- simplified geometry,
- painterly surfaces,
- atmospheric grading,
- less micro-detail.

### If too generic

Push toward:
- stronger shape language,
- clearer symbolic motifs,
- more biome-specific material identity.

### If too noisy

Push toward:
- fewer props,
- cleaner silhouette,
- stronger value grouping,
- calmer composition.

### If too abstract

Push toward:
- clearer traversable forms,
- stronger material legibility,
- clearer character/world scale.

---

## 8. Best First Pass Recommendation

If only one first pass is run, do this:

1. global world key art
2. urchin base character
3. Moss Cathedral scene
4. Marble Dunes flow descent
5. Sand Choir resonance reveal

Those five images alone can establish whether the project’s visual identity is working.

---

## 9. Final Recommendation

Use the runbook as a filter, not just a schedule.

The point of image generation here is not quantity.
It is to lock:
- identity,
- tone,
- material language,
- and movement readability.

The best generated images should feel like:

**this world was built for a tiny rolling pilgrim, and every shape in it knows how to sing back.**
