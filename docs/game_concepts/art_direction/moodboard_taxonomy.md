# Urchin Game Concept: Moodboard Taxonomy

**Status:** Planning taxonomy  
**Date:** 2026-04-24  
**Purpose:** Define the categories, search targets, and selection rules for building moodboards that support the game’s visual direction without drifting into realism or generic stylization.

---

## 1. Purpose

This taxonomy is for gathering references in a disciplined way.

It should help future sessions or artists collect imagery for:
- environment form,
- material language,
- lighting,
- character feel,
- VFX,
- color scripts,
- UI restraint,
- revelation-state intensification.

The goal is not to collect random pretty images.

The goal is to gather references that answer specific questions:
- What does this biome feel like?
- What makes this material readable but stylized?
- How do we create atmosphere cheaply?
- How can the urchin feel expressive without a face?
- What visual moves are reserved for revelation states?

---

## 2. Moodboard Collection Rules

### 2.1 Gather By Function, Not Only By Taste

Each image should justify itself as a reference for:
- shape,
- palette,
- material,
- atmosphere,
- motion,
- composition,
- or interaction readability.

### 2.2 Prefer Stylized References Over Realistic Targets

Reference categories should privilege:
- painterly environments,
- stylized materials,
- sculptural landforms,
- cinematic lighting,
- animated or game worlds with strong authorship.

Real-world references are useful mainly for:
- material behavior,
- landscape logic,
- scale,
- botanical structure,
- erosion patterns,
- light mood.

They should not become literal production targets.

### 2.3 Separate “Normal World” From “Revelation State”

Keep ordinary play references separate from:
- psychedelic intensification,
- sacred geometry,
- symbolic light behavior,
- world-as-song visual logic.

If these categories mix too early, the whole game can become visually muddled.

## 3. Primary Moodboard Categories

## 3.1 Shape Language Board

Purpose:
- define the macro forms of the world,
- keep terrain sculptural and readable,
- avoid noisy realism.

Look for:
- curved landforms,
- bowls,
- arches,
- root tunnels,
- ridges,
- suspended planes,
- natural forms with strong silhouette clarity.

Useful source types:
- stylized environment art,
- landscape sculpture,
- erosion photography used as abstraction,
- architectural shell forms,
- botanical macro structures.

Questions this board should answer:
- What shapes invite rolling?
- What shapes imply shelter?
- What shapes imply ascent?

## 3.2 Material Language Board

Purpose:
- define how materials should feel when stylized,
- avoid both realism and generic flatness.

Subcategories:
- moss and damp softness
- polished mineral
- sand and dust
- crystal and glass
- shallow reflective water
- membrane bloom tissue
- wind grass and reeds
- pearl / ceramic-organic shell for the urchin

Questions this board should answer:
- What is the minimum detail needed for a material to feel tactile?
- How much of the look should come from shader behavior versus texture detail?

## 3.3 Lighting And Atmosphere Board

Purpose:
- define mood through light,
- unify the stylized approach,
- show how beauty can come from air and color rather than detail density.

Look for:
- fog layers,
- bloom restraint,
- volumetric shafts,
- reflected color,
- dusk and dawn transitions,
- bioluminescent darkness,
- open sky elevation,
- diffuse sacred glow.

Questions this board should answer:
- How soft or sharp should the world feel?
- Where should contrast be held back?
- What makes a scene feel holy, lonely, or ecstatic?

## 3.4 Biome Palette Boards

Purpose:
- assign a color identity to each biome,
- avoid palette drift,
- ensure progression feels emotional.

One board per biome:
- Moss Cathedral
- Marble Dunes
- Sand Choir
- Glass Forest
- Bloom Caverns
- River Mirrors
- Wind Arches
- The Inner Sea

Questions this board should answer:
- What are the dominant hues?
- What are the accent hues?
- What emotional shift does this palette create relative to the prior biome?

## 3.5 Character Board

Purpose:
- define the urchin’s visual identity,
- control silhouette and material choices,
- support expression without face acting.

Look for:
- iconic rounded character forms,
- expressive non-facial animation references,
- shell and ceramic-organic materials,
- subtle glow logic,
- pilgrim-like emotional presence.

Questions this board should answer:
- How simple can the character remain while still feeling emotionally rich?
- How does the character remain visible in all biomes?

## 3.6 Motion FX Board

Purpose:
- define how movement leaves beauty behind it,
- connect visuals to tactile traversal.

Look for:
- dust trails,
- ripple propagation,
- pollen drift,
- resonance waves,
- water skim wakes,
- grass displacement,
- drifting seed motion,
- traveling glow.

Questions this board should answer:
- What does speed look like in this world?
- What does resonance look like?
- What makes movement feel satisfying and alive?

## 3.7 Device And Vehicle Board

Purpose:
- define how devices and vehicles belong to the world,
- prevent them from feeling mechanically disconnected.

Look for:
- sail forms,
- glider seed structures,
- resonant channels,
- ancient living machines,
- ceremonial rather than industrial mechanics,
- transport forms that feel like extensions of the world’s biology or forgotten culture.

Questions this board should answer:
- How do we make devices feel sacred, not utilitarian?
- How do vehicles feel like larger movement instruments instead of separate game modes?

## 3.8 Revelation-State Board

Purpose:
- reserve a clear visual language for rare visionary moments.

Look for:
- hidden geometry,
- sound-as-light,
- particle order,
- sacred patterning,
- impossible but clean spatial relationships,
- restrained surreal intensification.

Questions this board should answer:
- What changes only during revelation states?
- What must remain recognizable so the game still feels coherent?

## 4. Secondary Moodboard Categories

These are supporting boards, not the main style anchors.

### 4.1 Real-World Material Reference

Use for:
- moss softness,
- dune patterning,
- shallow water behavior,
- crystal transmission,
- fungal translucency,
- wind-blown grasses.

Purpose:
- anchor stylization in real behavior.

### 4.2 Composition And Camera Board

Use for:
- framing small characters in giant worlds,
- pilgrimage silhouettes,
- curved route composition,
- open-space release moments.

Purpose:
- ensure the game still looks strong in play, not just in concept pieces.

### 4.3 Symbol And Motif Board

Use for recurring visual motifs:
- circles,
- spirals,
- standing waves,
- nested arches,
- reflective symmetry,
- root-vein branching,
- rising paths.

Purpose:
- create visual recurrence without exposition.

### 4.4 Audio-Visual Coupling Board

This can include references where sound seems visible through:
- ripple logic,
- light waves,
- synchronized bloom,
- particle choreography.

Purpose:
- guide resonance visualization.

## 6. Character Moodboard Prompts

The urchin board should gather references for:
- iconic rounded creature forms,
- emotionally readable non-facial bodies,
- shell and ceramic materials,
- subtle glow seams,
- soft sacred objects,
- movement-based expression,
- pilgrim-like vulnerability and grace.

Avoid references that push the character toward:
- mascot cartoon energy,
- hard sci-fi drone design,
- overly mechanical complexity,
- plush-toy triviality,
- horror biomaterial.

The character should feel sincere and mysterious.

---

## 7. Good Reference Selection Criteria

A reference is strong if it clearly contributes to one of these:
- silhouette clarity,
- biome identity,
- material logic,
- emotional tone,
- movement readability,
- atmosphere,
- resonance visualization,
- late-game uplift.

A reference is weak if it is:
- just generally pretty,
- strongly realistic in a way the project cannot support,
- inconsistent with the game’s tone,
- visually loud but mechanically unreadable,
- cool in isolation but impossible to integrate.

---

## 9. Final Recommendation

Build moodboards with discipline.

The main target is not “stylized fantasy” in the abstract.
It is:

**a luminous painterly-sculptural pilgrimage world with readable materials, strong biome color identity, expressive environmental motion, and rare sacred-surreal intensifications.**

If every selected reference supports that sentence, the moodboard process is working.
