# Urchin Game Concept: Visual Direction Brief

**Status:** Planning brief  
**Date:** 2026-04-24  
**Purpose:** Define the overall visual style for the urchin game in a way that supports beauty, readability, emotional clarity, and tractable asset production.

---

## 1. Visual North Star

The recommended visual direction is:

**A luminous painterly-sculptural world built from simplified geometry, strong biome color scripts, material-reactive shaders, and restrained surreal intensification during rare revelation states.**

This should not aim for photorealism.

It should instead aim for:
- emotionally memorable environments,
- highly readable traversal spaces,
- tactile but stylized materials,
- beauty driven by shape, light, color, and motion,
- a production style that remains achievable without a realism pipeline.

---

## 2. What The Style Must Achieve

The visual style needs to do four jobs at once:

1. Make the world beautiful and transportive.
2. Make traversal and puzzle interaction readable.
3. Support the game's sincere, silent emotional tone.
4. Keep asset creation and worldbuilding tractable.

If a visual choice is beautiful but hurts navigation, it is wrong.  
If a visual choice is efficient but bland, it is also wrong.

The target is not realism. The target is **authored visual poetry with physical legibility**.

---

## 3. Core Principles

### 3.1 Shape First

Environments should be designed to read clearly in silhouette.

Prioritize:
- bowls,
- arches,
- ridges,
- root tunnels,
- suspended planes,
- curving channels,
- sculptural negative space.

The world should be readable before fine texture detail is added.

### 3.2 Material Suggestion Over Material Simulation

Materials should feel convincing without being literally realistic.

Examples:
- moss should read as soft, damp, and sheltering,
- sand should read as flowing, resonant, and wind-shaped,
- glass should read as fragile, luminous, and acoustically sharp,
- water should read as reflective, shallow, and graceful.

The goal is to convey the emotional and mechanical identity of a material, not to reproduce it physically in full fidelity.

### 3.3 Controlled Abstraction

The world should remain anchored enough to feel tactile and traversable, but stylized enough to feel authored and dreamlike.

Good target:
- believable material logic,
- simplified forms,
- painterly color transitions,
- occasional sacred-surreal exaggeration.

### 3.4 Light As Worldbuilding

Lighting should carry a major share of the game's beauty and mood.

Use:
- atmospheric depth,
- fog gradients,
- bloom accents,
- volumetric shafts,
- backlit particles,
- emissive pathways,
- reflected sky color.

Do not rely on dense geometry everywhere to achieve richness.

### 3.5 Motion As A Visual Layer

A large part of the world’s beauty should come from visible response:
- drifting pollen,
- swaying grasses,
- rippling sand,
- shimmer in shallow water,
- breathing blooms,
- traveling resonance glow,
- lifting dust,
- seed particles riding wind.

Static beauty is not enough for this game. The world should feel alive.

---

## 4. Recommended Style Formula

The most practical and compelling formula is:

**Simple geometry + strong color design + stylized materials + rich atmosphere + meaningful environmental motion**

This allows the project to:
- look distinct without realism,
- keep asset production under control,
- let shaders and lighting do meaningful work,
- preserve clear readability for traversal and puzzles.

---

## 5. Things To Avoid

Avoid these directions:

- full realism,
- scan-heavy naturalism,
- generic low-poly flat-shaded minimalism,
- overbusy noise textures,
- overly cartoon slapstick rendering,
- hyper-neon psychedelic visuals in normal play,
- UI-heavy visual communication replacing environmental readability.

This project should feel:
- lyrical,
- tactile,
- serene,
- occasionally uncanny,
- never visually cheap or generic.

---

## 6. Environment Style Rules

### 6.1 Terrain

Terrain should be:
- large-scale,
- smooth enough to support readable rolling lines,
- shaped like designed sculpture rather than raw erosion simulation,
- distinct per biome through profile and flow.

Recommended terrain logic:
- use broad forms first,
- let material treatment and set dressing add richness,
- ensure traversal routes are legible from camera-relevant angles.

### 6.2 Surface Materials

Use a limited number of master material families and reuse them intelligently across biomes.

Core families:
- soft organics,
- polished mineral,
- sand and dust,
- glass and crystal,
- shallow reflective water,
- luminous living tissue,
- wind-responsive grasses and reeds.

Each family should support:
- 2-4 palette variants,
- animated state changes,
- resonance-reactive response,
- readable traversal behavior.

### 6.3 Set Dressing

Set dressing should be iconic and clustered, not dense for its own sake.

Good examples:
- oversized seed pods,
- bloom membranes,
- crystal fans,
- root curtains,
- soft fungal towers,
- reed clusters,
- harmonic stone fins,
- luminous spore clouds.

Prefer repeated strong motifs over many weak unique props.

### 6.4 Distance And Horizon

Scale should come from composition and atmospheric layering, not from trying to fully detail every far zone.

Use:
- soft distant silhouettes,
- horizon forms,
- fog layers,
- suspended shapes,
- distant glow,
- huge but simple environmental masses.

---

## 7. Character Visual Direction

The urchin should be simple, iconic, and readable against rich environments.

It should not be over-textured.

Recommended character qualities:
- clean spherical silhouette,
- panel motion as primary expression,
- subtle glow channels or seam lighting,
- material state shifts tied to emotion or resonance,
- enough contrast to read in all biomes,
- elegant rather than cute-by-default.

The urchin should feel like some mix of:
- a sacred toy,
- a living artifact,
- a soft creature-machine hybrid,
- a tiny pilgrim body.

---

## 8. Rendering Style

### 8.1 Base Rendering

Recommended rendering feel:
- stylized physically readable materials,
- painterly gradients rather than noisy realism,
- clean forms with selective edge softness,
- clear highlight control on sculptural surfaces.

### 8.2 Post Processing

Post should unify the image without overpowering it.

Recommended:
- restrained bloom,
- fog color grading,
- subtle vignette in key scenes only,
- atmospheric depth,
- gentle chromatic richness in revelation moments only.

Avoid:
- constant heavy blur,
- exaggerated chromatic aberration,
- aggressive lens grime,
- realism tropes that do not fit the art style.

### 8.3 Effects

Effects should carry wonder and tactility.

Important categories:
- dust trails,
- pollen drift,
- resonance ripples,
- glow propagation,
- water shimmer,
- grass wake,
- seed loft,
- impact bloom.

Effects should help explain mechanics while remaining beautiful.

---

## 9. Revelation-State Visual Rule

Rare visionary interludes should look like intensified truth, not random visual chaos.

In these states:
- hidden geometry may appear,
- sound may become visible,
- particles may move with symbolic order,
- color ranges may widen,
- materials may feel more transparent to structure,
- resonance pathways may become luminous and interconnected.

These moments should be:
- rare,
- unmistakable,
- visually elevated,
- still compositionally clean.

They should not redefine the whole game’s look. They are punctuation, not baseline.

---

## 10. Production Tractability Rules

To keep this style achievable:

- Keep geometry broad and sculptural.
- Build biomes from reusable material systems.
- Let lighting and atmospherics do a lot of the richness work.
- Use motif repetition as a strength.
- Prefer hero moments over uniformly high detail.
- Keep the prop library smaller but more intentional.
- Invest in a few strong shader behaviors rather than trying to simulate everything realistically.

Do not build a pipeline that depends on:
- high-resolution scans,
- massive prop counts,
- dense realistic foliage everywhere,
- large numbers of unique texture sets for each sub-area.

---

## 11. Reference Orientation

The intended style should sit somewhere between:
- sacred landscape clarity,
- painterly environmental design,
- stylized tactile materials,
- animated-film color authorship,
- surreal but physically readable worldbuilding.

The target should feel like:
- a world you can roll through,
- a world you can read,
- a world you want to inhabit,
- a world that becomes more luminous as you understand it.

---

## 12. Final Recommendation

The visual direction should be locked as:

**Stylized, luminous, painterly-sculptural, materially readable, and emotionally sincere.**

Beauty should come from:
- shape,
- light,
- motion,
- material response,
- color script,
- atmosphere.

Not from realism.

That is the most interesting direction for the game and the most tractable one for production.
